When I'm Not Learning Code, Here's Where You Can Find Me!

Playing Hockey

I started my hockey career around the same time I started walking. I fell in love with the game through my dad, who played his whole life and was able to represent Team USA on the International stage!

I have been lucky enough to get the chance to represent Team USA in both the Sub-20 & Men's World Championships. I have captained the Team USA Sub-20 team twice before in our tournaments in Nanjing, China and Barcelona, Spain. In the last 2 years, the U.S. has placed 1st in the 2022 Challenger's Cup in San Juan, Argentina, and just recently placed 3rd in the 2024 Pan American Championships making history for this country! I will be forever grateful for this sport that has taken me to numerous continents, countries, and cities all over the world!

With this sport's main population being located in Europe, my coding adventure started with the hopes that I can get a job with the ability to work remote so I will be able to live anywhere in the world and play hockey!

Exercising

In order to compete at the highest level for hockey, physical fitness is a huge priority! In between all of the on-rink practices, athletes are encouraged to get in the gym and work on strength and conditioning. I am not the strongest guy in the world by any means, but it is important to keep your muscles toned and disciplined to maintain a good, healthy, competitive body for hockey!

One of my favorite exercises is running. For some odd reason, I enjoy placing headphones in my ears, turning on a good podcast or some good tunes, and just running to clear my mind. There is no better feeling than running outside on a nice day, getting some much needed fresh air, and enjoying life!

Enjoying a Beer With Friends

While work, school, and hockey are a huge part of my life, it is always good to kick back with a few of my best friends and enjoy a beer! Sometimes, the serotonin your body gets from socializing, cracking some jokes, and drinking a beer can really make your day!

Whether it is in the backyard around a nice bonfire, in a barstool at your favorite place, or in front of the tv watching your favorite sports team, there is no better feeling than drinking a beer and sharing some laughs with the most important people in your life.

Drinking in moderation is extremely important.
